Projects & impact

Delivering impact through projects,
partnerships and implementation.

Anchor works with development partners, impact organisations, governments and the private sector to design and implement projects that improve livelihoods, build capacity and unlock sustainable growth.

Many assignments are delivered under confidentiality agreements. The examples below are representative and, where appropriate, summarised or anonymised.

Our footprint

Anchor’s work spans Ghana and other African markets, connecting enterprises, investors, development partners and communities. Our projects combine advisory, capital, capacity building and programme delivery.

GIZ-ABF Matching Grant | ESMVC Project

Consortium partner with Dirsung Company Limited and Agrofredina Company Limited. Supported by GIZ ABF and co-funded by the EU, OACPS and BMZ.

  • Climate-smart maize value chain strengthening resilience in Northern Ghana
  • 200+ beneficiaries (50% women, 50% youth) across Bimbila, Gushiegu & Mion
  • Capacity building, digital innovation, market and finance access integrated

Impact Investing Ghana (IIGh)

  • Supported 30+ ESOs and SMEs across Ghana and Nigeria
  • Facilitated SCALE Quality Improvement Programme
  • Transaction advisory across agribusiness, education and energy sectors
  • Business audits, risk mitigation and investment readiness
  • Coaching under Deal Source Africa

USAID – Ghana Trade & Investment (GTI)

  • Supported export-ready agribusiness SMEs across non-traditional value chains
Results:
  • US$20m+ financing pipeline being facilitated
  • US$650,000 raised
  • 2 SMEs funded; 8 undergoing restructuring for funding

GIZ Health & NHIS Campaigns

Results:
  • 15,782 NHIS registrations
  • 21,591 health screenings
  • 121 health professionals trained
  • 100,000+ community members reached

Asafoods Ghana Ltd – Integrated Rice Project

  • Business development & coordination for 40-ton/day rice milling initiative
  • Enrolled under Mastercard HAPPY Project
  • 80 hectares cultivated
  • 100 youth farmers enrolled
